Recommended

Direction A, the keypad.

You told me this lives on a phone in a kitchen. That single fact rules out B — the moment the OS keyboard slides up it eats the calories field, and you're aiming at 6mm targets with fruit on your hands. C is genuinely nice for browsing, but it charges you a screen transition for every conversion, and you said you need this information easily. A puts the food, both numbers, and the keys on one screen that never moves.

On the numbers. All values are calories per 100 g of edible portion — what actually lands on the scale. The nine whole foods come from USDA FoodData Central. Three of them involved a judgment call worth knowing about:

Chicken tenders — 175, calculated, not looked up. Nothing in USDA matches your recipe, so this is built from it: 450 g raw tenderloin at 1.10 cal/g, plus the flour, egg, and panko that actually adhere (~70 g, ~207 cal — dredging throws most of it away), then ~22% water loss in the air fryer, which concentrates what's left. That's 715 cal over ~408 g finished. Confidence band is roughly 160–190; the swing is how heavily you bread them. Pan-frying these instead would put you near 220, because panko drinks oil.

Black beans — 118, brand-sensitive. That's USDA's regular canned, drained and rinsed. Low-sodium cans come in at 91. That's a 30% spread on the same food, so it's worth checking your can.

Canned corn — 81, drained. Drained, not packing liquid included — with the liquid it's about 62, since you're weighing mostly water. USDA itself carries a competing 67 figure for drained solids; if your can drains wet, lean low. Avocado uses 167 for Hass specifically, rather than the 160 all-varieties average.
